50-30-20: A Litmus Test for Financial Stability for Average Americans

50-30-20 is a rule of thumb to help people achieve financial stability through saving. We're told that if we just work hard, we can live the American Dream and move up. However, is this dream still achievable?

Scroll down to test the average American’s ability to follow the 50-30-20 rule.

The 50-30-20 Rule of Thumb

People will be in solid financial shape if they divide their monthly income:

50% on things they need, mandatory expenses like:

  • mortgage or rent
  • utilities
  • health care
  • groceries
  • transportation
  • childcare

30% on things they want, expenses like:

  • cable/internet/phone
  • dining out
  • entertainment
  • personal care
  • shopping
  • travel

20% on building their savings and paying down debt:

  • student loans
  • credit card debit
  • savings
  • retirement

So now what?

The big picture

These numbers look at the median. Imagine how hard it is for a household who's income is below the median. It’s almost impossible to live the American dream, save up and get ahead when it’s this hard to stay afloat as the average.

Why is this: It’s not that most Americans don’t make enough money, but it’s hard be responsible and save, when the bare necessities cost too much. The numbers just don’t add up. People are being squeezed by the high costs of housing, childcare, healthcare and their income can’t keep up. It’s not a simple problem with an easy fix.
