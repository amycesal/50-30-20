50-30-20: A Litmus Test for Financial Stability in American Households

The 50/30/20 rule is a rule of thumb to help people achieve financial security. Coined by Elizabeth Warren in her book, "All Your Worth", it lays down a simple-to-follow rule for households to prepare for the future and achieve long-term financial stability. But is it practical for real families?

Scroll down to learn about the rule, and test whether American households are able to follow it.

The 50/30/20 Rule of Thumb

The rule states that people will be in solid financial shape if they divide their monthly income like so:

50% of for things they need, mandatory expenses like:

  • housing
  • utilities
  • health insurance
  • groceries
  • childcare

30% for things they want, expenses like:

  • travel
  • concerts
  • sporting events
  • dinners out
  • hobbies

20% on building their savings and paying down debt:

  • emergency fund savings
  • retirement accounts
  • extra payments toward debt
